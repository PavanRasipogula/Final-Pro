Disease

Two spotted spider mite is a common pest that can cause damage to tomato plants. These tiny insects can be difficult to see with the naked eye, but their damage can be visible on the plant.

It is important to take action as soon as you notice a two spotted spider mite infestation to prevent the pests from spreading and causing further damage to the tomato plants.

Treatment:

  • Wash the tomato plant's leaves with a strong stream of water to dislodge the mites.
  • Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil to kill the mites. These are organic options that are effective in controlling two spotted spider mites.
  • Use chemical pesticides as a last resort if the infestation is severe. However, these should be used with caution, as they can harm beneficial insects and contaminate the environment.
  • Use resistant cultivars: Planting resistant tomato cultivars can prevent or reduce the incidence of the disease.
  • Introduce natural predators of the two spotted spider mites, such as ladybugs and lacewings, to the garden.
