Disease:

Late blight is a devastating disease that can affect tomatoes and other plants in the Solanaceae family, caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans. Once infected, the plant can rapidly develop dark, water-soaked lesions on leaves and stems, leading to plant death.

Some Prevention Strategies:
  • Plant resistant varieties
  • Practice good sanitation
  • Proper watering
  • Fungicides
  • Monitor your plants

Remember, prevention is the best strategy when it comes to late blight disease.

Treatment:

  • Remove infected plants: As soon as you notice the symptoms of late blight disease in your tomato plants, remove and destroy them immediately. Do not compost them as this can spread the disease.
  • Improve air circulation: Late blight disease thrives in humid environments, so make sure your tomato plants have adequate spacing between them to promote air circulation. Prune the plants to remove any overcrowded or diseased branches.
  • Water management: Avoid watering your tomato plants in the evening as this can create a humid environment that promotes the growth of late blight disease. Instead, water them early in the morning or during the day.
  • Fungicides: Use a fungicide that contains copper or chlorothalonil to prevent the spread of late blight disease in your tomato plants. Follow the instructions on the product label carefully.
  • Biological control: Use biological control methods such as introducing predatory insects like ladybugs or lacewings to your garden. These insects feed on the eggs and larvae of pests that can spread late blight disease.
