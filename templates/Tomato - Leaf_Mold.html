Disease

Leaf mold disease is a fungal disease that affects tomato plants. It is caused by the fungus Fulvia fulva (formerly known as Cladosporium fulvum) and is most common in humid and cool environments. The disease usually starts on the lower leaves of the plant and then spreads to the upper leaves.

Some Prevention Strategies:
  • Plant disease-resistant tomato varieties
  • Proper plant spacing
  • Water management
  • Proper pruning
  • Cleanliness
  • Fungicide application
  • Crop rotation

Remember, prevention is the best strategy when it comes to late blight disease.

Treatment:

  • Remove infected leaves: If you notice any leaves with mold, remove them immediately and dispose of them away from your garden.
  • Fungicides: Use a fungicide labeled for early blight disease and apply it according to the manufacturer's instructions. Repeat applications as necessary, usually every 7 to 10 days.
  • Keep plants dry: Avoid getting water on the leaves, as the moisture promotes the growth of the mold. Water the plants at the base of the stem rather than from overhead, and avoid watering during the evening.
  • Increase air circulation: Make sure that the plants are not crowded together, as this can create a humid environment that encourages the growth of the mold. Prune the plants to increase airflow between them.
  • Apply fungicides: There are several fungicides available that can be effective against leaf mold disease. Look for products that contain copper or chlorothalonil, and follow the instructions carefully.
  • Prevention: Leaf mold disease can be prevented by planting disease-resistant tomato varieties, maintaining proper spacing between plants, and avoiding overhead watering.
