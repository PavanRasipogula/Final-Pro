Disease

Tomato yellow leaf curl virus (TYLCV) is a plant disease that affects tomato plants. It is caused by a virus that is transmitted by a whitefly insect (Bemisia tabaci). The disease is characterized by yellowing and curling of the leaves, stunted growth, and reduced fruit production.TYLCV start to appear on the lower leaves of the tomato plant, and then gradually move up the plant. The leaves become yellow and curl upward, while the veins become thickened and the plant appears stunted. The affected fruit may be smaller, distorted, and have a mottled appearance.
